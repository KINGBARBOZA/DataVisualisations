New Zealand's average obesity weight has been an increasing concern over the past few years as more and more of their population begin to fall into the obese bracket of general health and weight. This has very negative health implications on the general population resulting in higher chances of heart diseases as well as diabetes within people affected by obesity. While this is seen as a growing trend within many first world countries which can be viewed within the graph above new Zealand has stood out among many nations as one of the worst cases of obesity within it's people. For the past 9 years New Zealand has remained on top of many counties with the highest recorded obesity rates. The main contributor to this problem is the poor eating habits found within the country and a lack of education behind the risks of obesity resulting in a far above acceptable levels of obesity within New Zealand. to combat this Zealanders should consider eating more healthy food choices which are more commonly done within Asian countries resulting in them having lower obesity rates as well as implementing more learning for kids and adults teaching the risks associated with a poor diet and obesity which would result in reducing obesity and the toll on health services within New Zealand. While this issue isn't unique to New Zealand and many more countries should do the same New Zealand is in a urgent needs to act now as obesity related deaths will only increase unless more measures and awareness is put in place to curve the increasing rates of obesity within their people.
